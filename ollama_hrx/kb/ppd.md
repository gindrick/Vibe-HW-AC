PPD 20 02

Frame orders – liquidation:

Guideline for liquidation

If the branches ask for liquidation of the decor they guarantee and HQ does not decide to include it in Hranipex collection decors, following rules are valid:
HQ will issue an invoice by standard way = resposibility of customer service
Information that these items are unsold / uncollected material related to frame orders which must be disposed of at HQ directly will be communicated via separate email = resposibility of customer service
The branches then post the Invoice to the Material disposal expense account = responsibility of the branch
Subsequently, physical liquidation will be carried out without any special protocol. HQ agrees physical liquidation with warehouse = responsibility of the customer service
The liquidation will be done without any surcharge as it used as a regenarate (production or sales) responsibility of the customer service


Yearly regular control of all open frame orders is recommended.

# Frame order rules Guideline


PPD 20 01

Frame orders - guideline:

Guideline for Guarantor.
The term Guarantor identifies the branch (for export and CZ sale representatives) that requested a specific article to be put and held on stock. This happens when an article was primarily evaluated for one branch, but HQ doesn’t consider it strategic for all other branches.
The Guarantor field in QAD (function 1.4.1) defines the responsibility
Turnover of the articles is evaluated annually.
If an article is ordered by at least two entities (excluding the Guarantor) and restocked twice, the goods is guaranteed by the HQ. This will be considered during the evaluation process. 
The articles are declared a Dead-stock after a safety period of 1 year.
Dead stock value will be deducted from the Branch revenue in the evaluation of bonuses.
Standard country abbreviations will be used to identify the Branch (CZ = Czech Branch, AT for Austria, DE for Germany etc.)

These rules also apply to goods under frame orders. In the same manner, whatever value of articles that was ordered by a Branch and not sold in 1 year period will be deducted from the revenue result of the Branch.

# PPD10 - Dead stock Guideline NEW July 20 NOT VALID


PPD 10 

Dead stock
The official Hranipex guideline for handling with dead stock at HQ:

Dead stock procedures are valid for following production lines:

1000,1010, 1110, 1111, 1210, 1310, 1410, 1510, 1610, 2020, 2120, 2220, 2320, 2420, 2520, 2720, 3030, 3130, 3230, 3330, 3430, 3530, 5220, 5320, 6221, 9899.

Procedures include:
Analysis will be done once per half a year. 
Everything what is above 18 months without movement in stock (including credit notes, returned goods, unplanned receipts etc.) is a potential dead stock and will be scrapped (excluding positions under the frame orders) = Hranipex Stock Collection.
X and V articles without jumbo rolls in stock will be scraped automatically (special cases will be solved individually)
Atypical prod. lines – 1111, 1310, 1410, 1510, 1610, 2120, 2320, 2520, 2720, 2920, 3330, 3430, 3530 – articles without  frame orders and amount bellow written will be scrapped automatically. 
ABS – 200 m and less than one jumbo roll
Melamine – 550 lm (11m2) and less than one jumbo roll
Veneer – less than one jumbo roll
Other articles with higher amount will be distributed to Back Office Managers (ESC).



If there is a „Guarantor“ of the item, the costs will be charged to the responsible branch. 
If there is no “Guarantor” then costs stay at HQ .
Purchasing department will inform Financial department so it can record dead stock costs.
Technical step QAD – scrapped items won´t be moved in ERP QAD into 9999 (mostly), but into atypical product lines (1310, 1510 etc.)

# PPD11 v6 - XXL BB Guideline


PPD 11 

XXL Orders 
The official Hranipex guideline for XXL orders – BB articles:

The XXL special price is applicable only for the edges that are manufactured in HRX Humpolec
The XXL special price can be applied only for a specific business case with a specific customer
XXL special price can´t be applied for generic big orders that would be stocked on the Branch and distributed to multiple customers
The XXL order shipment cant be split = no special shipment requests in the PDL. This means: the order is shipped in full volume to the branch who ensures potential partial shipments if needed
Storage of XXL orders in the HQ and partial shipments from the HQ are not allowed
Storage at the Branch: possible but only with a valid frame order
To apply for the special price the excel table „XXL Business Checklist“ has to be filled and sent to Ingmar Staudinger for the „Western“ Countries and Petr Zmatlo for the „Eastern“ Countries.
Volumes to qualify for the XXL special discount

1510 atypical product with stocked MasterBatch (line 9090) = D+10
1510 atypical product with non-stock MasterBatch (line 9091) = D+20
1510 atypical product with non-stock MasterBatch which should be slitted after production = D+25
XXL business that would require colourmatch – always on demand

PPD14 BA 
Edgings - allowed bending or edgebanding of radii 
This information describes behavior of edgings with respect to their potential cracking or 
changing structure or color (stress whitening) during radii edgebanding.

1.1 
plastic ABS edges 
Plastic edgings can be used also for banding curves, radii. In the table 
below are allowed, parameters which are tested using a simple bending 
test on a fixture according to the declared radius.

wall of silver part of T-square. (See picture 1 and 2). Those edges which exceed 3 mm in the

PPD14  
Longitudinal (Machine Direction) cracking of thin edges 
This information describes behaviour of thin edgings with respect to their potential cracking 
in Machine Direction (hereinafter MD). 
1 
Cracking of thin edgings (mainly 0,5mm) in MD 
Molecules of ABS polymer are oriented in MD during exit of the polymer melt from the extrusion 
die during production of ABS Plain Colour Edgings and ABS Natural Finish Edgings. This 
orientation is fixed during cooling of the material. This process creates anisotropic mechanical 
properties of the final edgings (different mechanical properties in Machine and Transverse direction 
(hereinafter TD)) 
 
Produced edgings can be bended in TD without any problem but when bended in MD it easily 
cracks (as depicted in the pictures bellow) alongside oriented molecules Thin edgings 
(approx. 0,5 - 0,8 mm) are most prone to this behaviour. Thicker edgings are more resistant due to 
bigger wall thickness that compensates for this phenomenon. 
  
This behaviour is typical for production process of ABS edgings and is not understood as product 
failure. Thin ABS edgings (either blocks or final edges) are not intended for bending in MD and is 
therefore essential to set up further processes to eliminate any MD bending.

V tomto dokumentu je popsáno chování tenkých hran s ohledem na jejich křehkost

1 
Praskání tenkých hran (převážně 0,5mm) v podélném směru 
Při výrobě nábytkových hran ABS hrany UNI barvy nebo ABS hrany Přírodní Dekor dochází při 
výstupu taveniny z hubice vytlačovacího stroje k orientaci molekul polymeru ABS ve směru 
vytlačování. Tato orientace se následně zafixuje při chlazení materiálu. Tímto dochází ke vzniku 
anizotropických vlastností ve finálním produktu nábytkové hrany (tj. dochází ke vzniku rozdílného 
chování a vlastnosti materiálu ve směru podél a napříč).  
Vyráběná hrana je odolná na ohyb v příčném směru, ale při ohybu ve směru podél dochází k jejímu 
relativně snadnému praskání ve směru orientace molekul. Tento efekt se nejvíce projevuje 
u tenkých hran (cca 0,45 - 0,8 mm). U silnějších hran je tento efekt částečně kompenzován větší 
tloušťkou stěny výrobku a praskání se neprojevuje tak často.  
 
Toto chování je charakteristické pro výrobní proces ABS plastových hran a není vadou výrobku. 
Z tohoto důvodu nejsou bloky a plastové hrany určeny k ohýbání a olepování křivek v podélném 
směru. Proto je důležité nastavit procesy olepování a zacházení s hranami tak, aby bylo co nejvíce 
zabráněno jejich podélném ohybu.

Melamine edges – unpacking procedure 
1 
Document purpose 
Herewith we described right method of unpacking melamine edges. This way of 
unpacking prevents unrolling and damaging of edgings. 
2 
Melamine edgings  
The melamine edgings are made from paper saturated by the resin. The roll of this edgings is 
not possible to secure by the bands. Using band surrounding roll causes damage of edging. 
2.1 
Melamine edgings packaging 
After their slitting is end of every edging fixed by the paper tape and label on every roll 
separately and all slit edgings are fixed stretch foil directly on machine as one compact 
package. Rims of compact block are fixed by the carton. 
 
If every edging is packed separately ends of edging is fixed by the paper tape and label and 
put into paper box. 
2.2 
Corect unpacking of block of melamine edges 
Below in the pictures is presented the right procedure of unpacking of melamine edgings. 
Breach of presented way of unpacking lead to spontaneous unrolling and damaging 
edges and potential claims which occurred wrong way of unpacking will not be taken 
into account.

New marking of rolls stocked in HQ warehouse without boxes
Dear colleagues, 
We´d like to inform you that from Mid of July 2019 we´ll start to use new marking of rolls which will be stocked in HQ warehouse without boxes, just separated rolls fixed by tapes only.
We will use this solution from internal logistic reasons to save the costs, we will apply it mainly on low sold positions.

# PPD14 v2- Slitting line R24 - guideline


PPD 14 v2


SLITTING LINE R24:

Dears, 
we would like to inform you about our new slitting line R24 that allows us to slit thin ABS edges (0,45 – 0,7 mm) and melamine (standard/FALZ) edges:

“Salami” slitting technology
Minimum width to be slit = 16 mm
Maximum width to be slit = 200 mm
Inner core = 300 mm








Production possibilities:



Attention:
New conditions for slitting are valid. Thin ABS edges will be slit only on slitting line R24. Miminum taking amount is 200m.
Conditions for slitting thick ABS edges do not change.
When slitting melamine edges a minimum quantity starts on 100m, the old slitting line is used (several shorter rolls). In case of slitting on line R24, min. taking amount is 200m and then multiples up to a full jumbo roll length depending on customer´s order. It means in case of order 200m and its multiples, the slitting line R24 will always be used. For example: order 400m will be processed on slitting line R24 (2 rolls a´ 200m). In case of order 450m, old slitting line is used (several rolls).

1 
Bělení (Stress whitening) ABS hran 
Stress Whitening je jev vlastní většině polymerů – ABS nevyjímaje. Tento defekt se projevuje, když 
je materiál vystaven napětí/namáhání, v případě olepování ABS hran při jejich ohýbání nebo 
strojním opracování po nalepení. Aplikované napětí při těchto procesech, změní rozložení molekul 
polymeru (v místě napětí dochází k tvorbě mikro-trhlinek, orientace řetězců a vznik mikro děr) díky 
čemuž vzniknou linky bílé barvy. Tyto bílé linky mohou být viditelné napříč na hraně, při jejím 
ohýbání, nebo v podélném směru při opracování rádiusů při olepování desek hranou (na 
opracovávaném rádiusu 2 mm a větším). Vhodné nastavení nástrojů užitých pro opracování, 
jednoznačně redukuje toto probělení při zpracovávání hran. 
Tento efekt probělení hrany není nezvratný (zejména v případě bílých linek vzniklých při 
opracování při olepování) a lze jej téměř odstranit použitím zdroje tepla a zahřátím proběleného 
povrchu, což pomáhá návratu molekul do původní podoby. Pro toto nahřátí lze použít 
horkovzdušnou pistoli nebo dokonce obyčejný fén na vlasy. Je též možné a účinné zaleštění bílých 
míst ručně nebo vhodným nástrojem. V případě použití horkovzdušné pistole nebo nástroje 
k zaleštění, je však nezbytné věnovat pozornost tomuto procesu, a zabránit možnému přehřátí 
nebo spálení hrany nebo lepidla působením přílišné teploty nebo doby aplikace zdroje tepla. 
Společnost Hranipex se zaměřuje na výrobu hran s minimalizací efektu bělení. Je však nemožné 
odstranit tento efekt ze 100 %, ale lze pouze učinit taková opatření, aby byl efekt bělení 
minimalizován. Neboť efekt bělení je přirozená vlastnost polymeru.

Stress Whitening of ABS edges 
This document described common physical property of ABS edges with explanation and 
instructions for eliminating undesirable issues. 
1 
Stress Whitening (hereinafter SW) of ABS edge bands Plastic edges 
Stress Whitening is intrinsic behavior of majority of polymers – ABS included. This appears when 
the material is exposed to the stress – bending or machine processing in case of ABS edge bands. 
Applied stress makes molecules of polymer to reorganize (creating of micro-holes and locally 
oriented areas) resulting in visible white lines. These white lines can be visible across the width of 
the edge in case of bending it or alongside of machined surface (radius milling of 2 mm and thicker 
edges). Proper setting of machining tools can significantly reduce SW effect of processed edges. 
Visual effect of SW is not irreversible (especially in case of white lines after machine processing) 
and can minimized by applying of heat to the surface – enabling molecules to reorganize back. It 
can be done either by hot air gun or even hair dryer or even simply by manual or machine polishing 
of the edge (please pay attention not to overheat or burn the edge or the glue by applying too much 
heat).  
Hranipex is providing edge band with focus on minimized SW effect. But it is necessary to mention 
that this effect can be more or less successfully masked but cannot be 100% removed as it is 
property of polymer itself. 
1.1 
SW after slitting 
The same SW there is visible after slitting of the edges. This is caused by the reasons described 
in previous chapter. Slitting procedure is done on slitting machines by the circular knives. These 
knives pre-cutt plastic edge and then it is split by the press and edge is banded. Due to this technical 
procedure there is visible white print on the fracture.

Basic material humidity 
Absolute humidity of supplied veneer edges is 12 ± 3 %.  
1.1.1 Humidity measurement 
The veneer edges humidity is measured by a moisture meter with accuracy of ± 2 %. The 
measured value is given as the arithmetic mean derived from three measures. In case of doubt 
must be a weight test carried out according to the special standard.   
1.1.1.1 
Possible problems 
In the case of higher humidity than the allowed value it could be problems with processing. 
The thick edge with high humidity could separate during processing.  If problems occur, the 
veneer edges must be stored in the propriate way until absolute humidity is stabilized. 
1.2 
Correct storage of veneer edges 
The veneer edges should be stored always horizontally in a dry airy place with a stable 
temperature 18 – 22oC and the relative humidity of air 40 – 60 %. The storage place should be 
protect against bad weather conditions. The storage temperature, relative humidity and 
weather conditions must be regularly controled and if the parameters are out of range they 
must be regulated back to the given values and the humidity of veneer edges must be 12 ± 3 % 
as a maximum humidity before processing. 
The veneer edges shall be stored in appropriate places, on shelves, which are 10 cm above 
the ground as a minimum and shall be protected from moisture. Do not expose the edges to 
sunlight or other UV rays as this might cause color changes of the veneer. Avoid direct contact 
with glue, fat or lubricants as this might affect the processability.
